  • 1920-1950s

    Golden decades of smoking

    Also WWII – companies proudly advertised their shipments of custom packed cigarettes to soldiers in the war.

    This was a time of increased consumption in smoking – where 50% of women were now smoking – in addition to 75-80% of men.

  • 1950s

    Led to a cancer scare: A German article linking smoking to lung cancer was published and Reader’s Digest picked it up. It was published in 1952 Cancer by the Carton. This article began the opening of public thinking about health concerns related to smoking.

    Following this article, cigarette companies responded to public concerns about lung cancer and cigarettes by putting filters on cigarettes to minimize the toxins.
